Flat Mirrors and The Law of Reflection


Law of Reflection in action

In the diagram, the blue line labeled "I" represents a ray of light approaching the flat mirror. This ray of light is known as the Incident Ray. The line "R" is the light that is reflected off of the mirror, which is known as the Reflected Ray. At the point at which the light strikes the surface of the mirror, a perpendicular line "N" can be drawn. It is called the Normal Line, which divides the angle between the incident and reflected ray into two angles. The angle between the Incident Ray and the Normal Line is called the Angle of Incidence. The angle between the Reflected Ray and the Normal Line is called the Angle of Reflection. They can also be written as "i" (theta-i) or "r" (theta-r), respectively. According to the Law of Reflection, when a ray of light reflects off of a surface, the angle of incidence is equal to the angle of reflection.
