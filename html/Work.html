Answers (Don't Cheat!)



1. The sun at 7 PM shines a ray of light that reflects off of the window of the house, which the observer sees. However, the sun at 1 PM does not have a ray of light that reaches the window of the house, and instead shines onto the ground.

2. Two times (once off the left-most mirror and then once more off the right-most mirror).

3. The angle at which the ray hits the reflective surface (angle of incidence) will equal the angle at which the ray departs the reflective surface (angle of reflection).

4. D. As the angle of incidence approaches 90 degrees, the angle between the reflected ray and the normal also approaches 90 degrees. This means that the total angle between the reflected and incident rays will approach 180 degrees.

5.

In order to complete this problem, one must draw normal lines perpendicular to the reflective surfaces at each point where the rays hit. The angle of incident should always equal the angle of reflection, which is the law of reflection. Draw the proceeding rays based on this rule.

6. Given that the incident rays reflecting off of a concave mirror converge at the focal point, the light can be concentrated at an object at the focal point of the mirror, which will in turn heat the object depending on how strong the light source is. 7. Convex mirrors have wider fields of view compared to other types of mirrors such as the concave mirror. This is a result of their tendency to reflect diverging rays of light.

8. Sorry, this was a trick question! A metallic spoon is an example of both because either side could be a mirror. The front side that curves in is a concave mirror and the back side that curves out is a convex mirror.
